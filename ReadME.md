# OpenHIDLab – Supporting Research Documents

This directory contains the Final Year Project (FYP) proposal for **OpenHIDLab** along with the supporting research documents prepared during the project's initial investigation phase.

The objective of these studies was to evaluate the current state of gaming peripherals, USB Human Interface Devices (HIDs), embedded firmware ecosystems, industry practices, commercial products, and academic research in order to identify meaningful engineering gaps and assess the feasibility of the proposed project.

The findings from these documents directly influenced the scope, architecture, and research direction presented in the accompanying proposal.

# Purpose of These Documents

These documents were prepared as supporting material during the project ideation and feasibility study. Collectively, they helped answer the following questions:

- What solutions already exist?
- What limitations do current solutions have?
- Which engineering problems remain insufficiently addressed?
- Where is the research and market gap?
- Is the proposed project technically feasible within the scope of a Final Year Project?
- How does the proposed solution compare with existing commercial and open-source alternatives?

The insights gathered from these studies were used to refine the project scope and develop the attached proposal for **OpenHIDLab**.

---

# Proposed Project

**OpenHIDLab: An Open Hardware and Software Platform for Characterization and Validation of USB Human Interface Devices**

The proposed project aims to develop an integrated embedded platform consisting of custom hardware, modular firmware, and desktop software capable of objectively characterizing, calibrating, and validating USB Human Interface Devices (HIDs) through repeatable engineering measurements.
